1. Problem Statement 
Residents of shared housing often share bills, such as rent, water, electricity, internet, food, and 
other household expenses. Managing shared bills can be difficult if: 
• Each person pays for different things at different times. 
• Some residents may have outstanding bills. 
• Utilities (water, electricity, etc.) are charged at a flat rate and do not take individual 
consumption into account. 
• Consumptions can vary from person to person. 
• Manual billing leads to confusion, conflicts, and inefficient solutions. 
• Current cost-sharing systems often rely on fair distribution and do not always reduce the 
number of transactions required to pay the bill. 
Therefore, it is important to implement legislation that offers flexible cost-sharing options and 
improves payment relationships among residents. 
2. Objectives 
The main objectives of the project are: 
• Design and develop a system for recording and managing household expenses. 
• Select different payment methods: 
• Equal distribution (everyone pays the same amount). 
• Distribution is based on acceptable use. (Weighted distribution) 
• Calculate the average cost per person (the amount that must be paid). 
• Develop a payment system that minimizes the number of transactions required to repay a 
debt. 
• Provide settlement instructions that clearly indicate who is required to pay and how 
much. 
3. Technologies to be used

Backend: PHP

Hosting: Hostinger 
4. Expected Outcomes 
• At the completion of the project, the system is expected to: 
• Accurately record shared expenses among individuals living in a shared space 
• Allow flexible selection of either equal or weighted cost sharing 
• Automatically calculate each person's share 
• Track outstanding balances over time 
• Generate an optimized settlement plan 
• Reduce conflicts and confusion in shared financial management.
